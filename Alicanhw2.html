Terminology and Some Information about Electricity Market Prices

  • Key idea behind the electricity markets is âbalanceâ.
  • Some of the markets are named as:
  1. Day Ahead Market - DAM (GÃ¼n Ãncesi PiyasasÄ± - GÃP)
  2. Intraday Market - IDM (GÃ¼n Ä°Ã§i PiyasasÄ± - GÄ°P)
  3. Balancing Power Market - BPM (Dengeleme GÃ¼Ã§ PiyasasÄ± - DGP).
  • Electricity market (in Turkey) is hourly.
  • MCP means âMarket Clearing Priceâ and âSMP means System Marginal Priceâ and MCP and SMP abbreviations will be used,respectively, to refer them.
  • SMP is always higher than MCP if system has Energy Deficit, and lower if there is Energy Surplus. Market operator also penalizes the operations in BPM by 3%. This is called Imbalance Price. Negative (Deficit) Imbalance Price is calculated as max(MCP,SMP)1.03 and Positive Imbalance Price is calculated as min(MCP,SMP)0.97.
